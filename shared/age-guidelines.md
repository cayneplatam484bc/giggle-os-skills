# Age-Appropriate Content Guidelines

Giggle OS targets kids ages 5-10. Adapt all content based on age group.

## Age 5-6 (Kindergarten)

**Reading level:** Short sentences, simple words, 1-2 lines max
**Interaction:** Large tap targets (56px+), no typing required, one action at a time
**Difficulty:** No fail states, no losing, everything is encouraging
**Content:** Bright colors, lots of emoji, frequent rewards, no scary elements
**Math:** Counting, basic addition (1-10)
**Science:** Single fact sentences, familiar animals and objects

Example text: "Tap the star!"
Example feedback: "Great job! You did it! ⭐"

## Age 7-8 (Early Elementary)

**Reading level:** Paragraphs of 2-3 sentences, some new vocabulary
**Interaction:** Standard tap targets (48px), simple text input OK, 2-3 choices
**Difficulty:** Gentle progression, lives/retries, mistakes are learning moments
**Content:** Can include mild mystery or suspense, introduce cause-and-effect
**Math:** Addition, subtraction, simple multiplication
**Science:** 2-3 sentence explanations, introduce science terms with context

Example text: "The brave cat looked around the dark cave. What should Whiskers do?"
Example feedback: "Almost! Try again — you're getting closer!"

## Age 9-10 (Upper Elementary)

**Reading level:** Full paragraphs, real vocabulary, more complex narratives
**Interaction:** Smaller controls OK (44px min), typing and complex input, multiple simultaneous options
**Difficulty:** Real challenge, high scores, timed elements, strategic thinking
**Content:** Can include suspense, competition, complex branching, educational depth
**Math:** All basic operations, fractions intro, patterns
**Science:** Short paragraphs with real terminology, data and comparisons

Example text: "Mercury orbits the Sun at an average distance of 57.9 million km. Because it's so close to the Sun, temperatures can reach 430°C during the day!"
Example feedback: "Score: 2,450 — New personal best!"

## Universal Rules (All Ages)

- **No violence:** Characters don't die, they "go away" or "try again"
- **No fear:** Nothing genuinely scary; spooky is OK for 9-10 if playful
- **Positive endings:** Every story ends happily
- **Inclusive:** Diverse characters, no stereotypes
- **Safe:** No external links, no data collection, no chat
- **Encouraging:** Mistakes are always framed as learning, never failure
- **Accessible:** High contrast text, clear visual hierarchy
